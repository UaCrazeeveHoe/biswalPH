JUAN LUNA

Spoliarium

About the Artist

Juan Luna (1857–1899) was a Filipino painter, sculptor, and political activist. His works often reflected themes of nationalism and social justice.

About the Artwork

Spoliarium (1884) is Luna’s most famous painting, depicting fallen gladiators being dragged into a chamber. It symbolizes the oppression of Filipinos under Spanish rule.

Why We Chose This

This artwork embodies Filipino resilience and the struggle for freedom, making it a cornerstone of national identity.

Preservation & Threats

Currently housed in the National Museum, the painting requires ongoing conservation to protect it from aging and environmental damage.
